Energy transition

I was introduced to this project while working at Teamstratenwerth in Basel, Switzerland. My responsibility was to illustrate and give life to an already defined storyline, taking in consideration the existing brand guidelines.

Energiewend is a moving exhibition that was first opened in Munich at the Deutsches Museum. The aim of this exhibit is to allow visitors to learn more about the types of energies that exist as well as their impact on our society.

My process

Goal

The goal of the project was to create four animations to inform visitors about issues such as emissions trading, nuclear energy, storage of nuclear waste and the concept of smart homes.

Participation

As the storyline was already defined, I started thinking what kind of visuals could represent the story told while still following the brand guidelines already set. The elements in the video were kept simple and centralized so that viewers could focus and still see the seamless flow from one frame to another.

Conclusion

These four videos were available at screen stations in different sections of the exhibition and served to give visitors a short overview of issues on how we, as a society, deal with energy and its byproducts.

This series of animations were a small part of a big and well orchestrated exhibit, but their importance lies on the powerful information they make public.
